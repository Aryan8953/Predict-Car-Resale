# Teacher's Viva Questions 

### Q1. Why do we use head()?
To view the first few records and verify that the dataset has loaded correctly.

### Q2. What does shape return?
It returns the number of rows and columns in the dataset.

### Q3. Why is info() useful?
It displays column names, data types, non-null values, and memory usage.

### Q4. Why do we use describe()?
It provides summary statistics of numerical columns, helping us understand the data distribution.

# Viva Questions

### Q2. Why check duplicate records?
Duplicates can bias the model and distort analysis.

### Q3. Why use a histogram?
To visualize the distribution of a numerical feature.

### Q4. Why use a boxplot?
To detect outliers and understand the spread of the data.

### Q5. What does a scatter plot show?
The relationship between two numerical variables.

### Q6. What is correlation?
Correlation measures the strength and direction of the relationship between two numerical variables, with values ranging from -1 to +1.
